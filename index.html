    Projects

    Benchmarking and Optimizing Enhanced Natural Convection Heat Sinks

    Simulation-based evaluation and optimization of vertical-fin heat sink designs for passive thermal management in electronics. Using validated CFD models and comparative literature analysis, optimized straight and displacement fin geometries achieved improved heat transfer performance while maintaining equal volume and base temperature conditions. Results support a standardized evaluation framework for enhanced natural convection designs in compact passive cooling applications.

    PortaVax: Design and Development of Portable Vaccine Cooler for Storage and Transport

    PortaVax is a lightweight, power-free vaccine cooler designed for last-mile delivery in remote regions. Using ATP-6 phase change material and layered insulation, it maintains temperatures below 8°C upto 25 hours. With a capacity of 840 vials and an ergonomic backpack-style frame, PortaVax offers rugged performance, comfort, and affordability for critical medical outreach.

    Experimental Evaluation of Advanced UAV Noise Reduction Technology

    Experimental evaluation of advanced UAV noise reduction technology involved aerodynamic and acoustic optimization of drone propeller blades using novel design modifications. Trailing-edge serrations with anhedral eagle winglets achieved a 31.96% increase in thrust and a 21.2% reduction in high-frequency noise, validated through 3D-printed prototypes and precision thrust stand testing.

    Vehicle Optimization for Sustainable Mobility

    A 2018 Mazda 6 Skyactiv-G was simulated on the FTP-75 drive cycle to evaluate baseline fuel consumption and CO₂ emissions. Optimization strategies included refining the RPM shift schedule, reducing vehicle mass, improving aerodynamic drag, and tuning gear ratios to balance efficiency and performance. These combined measures achieved up to a 25.9% reduction in emissions compared to the baseline configuration, highlighting the effectiveness of integrated vehicle optimization.

    Wind Resource Assessment and Offshore Suitability

    MATLAB was used to analyze offshore wind energy potential using buoy data from Irish sites M2 and M4, with the developed script adaptable to other locations. Statistical methods included data distribution analysis, Weibull and Rayleigh probability fits, and wind speed extrapolation to 100 m hub height. Turbine suitability was evaluated using the Siemens-Gamesa SG 10.0-193 DD model, demonstrating both the feasibility of the sites and the adaptability of the analysis framework for offshore wind assessment.

    CryoSync: Design and Development of Graphics Card Cooler

    CryoSync is a next-gen liquid cooling solution for high-performance GPUs like the RTX 3090. It combines jet impingement heat transfer with adaptive pump control to maintain GPU temperatures below 36°C while reducing energy use by up to 85% under low-load conditions. The modular top plate enables user customization, and its die-cast aluminum build ensures efficient, scalable manufacturing.

    Reinventing Porters Value Chain

    Reinvented Porter’s Value Chain by integrating sustainability, digitalization, and ethics to address gaps in traditional models such as SCOR and DEA. Developed a benchmarking tool with activity-level assessments and dashboards to measure sustainability and digitalization, enabling companies to enhance resilience, ensure ethical compliance, and improve competitiveness in global supply chains.

    Design and Development of Novel Heat Exchanger Shapes

    ThermoShelf is a dual-purpose hydronic radiator that combines high-efficiency heating with modular shelving for modern living spaces. Designed to deliver up to 1000W of thermal output via natural convection, it replaces negative wall space with functional storage. Its aluminum heat exchanger and mild steel frame are engineered for manufacturability and cost-efficiency, while customizable shelving enables flexible use across homes, offices, and hotels.

    Experimental Verification of Prototype Domestic Wet Radiator

    Experimental verification of a prototype domestic wet radiator combining efficient heat transfer with a visually striking guitar-shaped design. The system was fabricated from aluminum and copper, tested across varying flow rates and inlet temperatures, and analyzed through both experimental and CFD methods. Results showed a peak heat transfer of 412 W at 6 L/min, with clear thermal gradients observed via infrared imaging. Deviations between CFD and physical testing highlighted the impact of real-world manifold losses and non-uniform flow distribution.

    Design and Fabrication of HDPE Pipe Welding Machine

    This HDPE Pipe Welding Machine was custom-built for C. Vishnu Plastic Welds to enable efficient butt fusion welding of polyethylene pipelines. It features a rack-and-pinion driven clamping system, a precision-controlled heating plate, and an adjustable frame to accommodate various pipe diameters. Designed for reliability and portability, the machine delivers strong, uniform welds compliant with ASTM F2620 standards and significantly reduces setup and operation time for field technicians.
